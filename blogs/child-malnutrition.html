Exploratory Data Analysis on Child Malnutrition

EDA ¬∑ Public Health ¬∑ Nutrition ¬∑ Kenya

Project Overview

Child malnutrition remains one of the most critical public health challenges in developing countries. This project focuses on exploring child malnutrition indicators to understand underlying patterns, risk factors, and disparities.

Using real-world health and demographic data, the analysis applies exploratory data analysis (EDA) techniques to uncover trends related to nutrition outcomes among children under five years.

Research Questions

  • What is the prevalence of child malnutrition across different regions?
  • How do socio-economic and health factors relate to malnutrition indicators?
  • Are there observable trends over time?

Dataset Description

The dataset consists of child nutrition and health indicators collected from national health surveys and public health sources.

  • Target Indicators: Stunting, wasting, underweight
  • Demographics: Age, gender, region
  • Health Factors: Immunization, maternal health
  • Socio-economic Variables: Household conditions, access to healthcare

Methodology

  1. Data cleaning and handling missing values
  2. Univariate analysis of malnutrition indicators
  3. Bivariate analysis with socio-economic factors
  4. Visualization using charts and summary statistics

Key Findings

  • Stunting emerged as the most prevalent form of malnutrition
  • Rural regions showed higher malnutrition rates than urban areas
  • Children from low-income households were disproportionately affected
  • Access to healthcare and maternal education showed protective effects

Insights & Implications

The analysis highlights the strong connection between socio-economic conditions and child nutrition outcomes. Addressing malnutrition requires not only food security interventions but also improvements in healthcare access, education, and maternal support programs.

Tools & Technologies

  • Python
  • Pandas & NumPy
  • Matplotlib & Seaborn
  • Jupyter Notebook
